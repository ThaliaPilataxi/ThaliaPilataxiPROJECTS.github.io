Parking Garage - Kop van Boulevard

The project of Module 4 was dedicated to the design of a new parking garage for the municipality of Enschede, which also consisted of storage units. Four important aspects where considered for the design of the parking garage: (1) System Engineering; (2) Structural Design; (3) Circular Economy and Sustainability; and (4) Finance. For more details check the report here.

Design of miter gates and a pedestrian bridge

Module 6 was dedicated to design a convenient, safe, efficient and sustainable system which should function in a fully energy-neutral manner. Therefore, the project was divided in two parts: the first part was focused on the design method and strategy, and structural mechanics. The second pat was focused on the environmental and economic sustainability, social sustainability, and energy use in the build environment. For more technical details see the report of the first part here and a poster of the complete project here.

Vegetation monitoring with remote sensing

Module 9 of my bachelor studies was dedicated to creating a physical scale model of a multifunctional flood defence by an interdisciplinary team in which each member studied a specific topic in-depth. I studied remote sensing vegetation management by using satellite data with Google Earth Engine. The information was used to develop land-use classification maps and vegetation management strategies. More details can be found in the report here.

Smart Infrastructure Exploration

There is uncertainty about the exact location of onderground utilities, since different parties such as municipalities and utility companies manage different information about their exact location. Therefore, in Module 10 a smart city engineering project was carried out, which was focused on how to elaborate a 3D model of such utilities considering the uncertainty of their exact location. Such project should help to determine the exact location of utilities, in case there is conflicting information between the different parties. More details can be found here.

Paving and Compaction Support Systems - The status of implementation worldwide

This research focused on reporting the state-of-the-art of paving and compaction support systems worldwide. Therefore, their evolution and introduction within road construction regulations were studied. The retrieved information served to analyse the enablers and barriers to their implementation. Finally, recommendations for the fast-tracked implementation of these high-tech solutions were elaborated. The thesis can be found here.
